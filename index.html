1. MVC — Maximum Voluntary Contraction
Highest force patient can voluntarily produce without movement. Represents maximum muscle strength.
2. Peak Force
Highest force value recorded during the contraction session.
3. Time to Peak Force (TTP)
Time from contraction start to maximum force. Indicates muscle response speed.
4. RFD — Rate of Force Development
RFD = ΔF/Δt (N/s). How fast the muscle generates force. Key indicator of neuromuscular power.
5. Fatigue Index (FI)
FI = (Initial − Final) / Initial × 100%. Higher FI means muscle fatigues faster.
6. Force Impulse
Impulse = ∫F dt (N·s). Area under force-time curve. Represents total effort/work produced.
7. Force–Time Graph
The live graph above shows Force (N) plotted against Time. It shows contraction pattern: how fast force rises, how long it is maintained, and how it declines.

Our Story

Biomedionics was established in Narowal, Punjab, Pakistan — with the goal of bridging the gap between expensive imported medical devices and the real needs of local clinics, hospitals, and research institutions.

Our team of biomedical engineers and healthcare professionals work together to design devices that combine cutting-edge technology with practical, field-tested reliability. Every product is built and validated in Pakistan, for Pakistan — and for the global market.

From our flagship Isometri Muscle Meter — which streams real-time force data via Firebase — to our BioSoft Ⅰ bioprinter and Diabe-Neurosense neuropathy diagnostic system, we are committed to raising the standard of medical technology in our region.

📡 Device Data & Firebase

When you connect your Isometri Muscle Meter, real-time force measurement data is transmitted to Google Firebase (a Google LLC service) and stored in our Realtime Database. This data is associated with your Device ID (MAC address) and is accessible only through your unique Device ID.

You can disconnect your device at any time to stop data transmission. Historical reading data may be stored in Firebase for up to 30 days.

🛡️ Data Security
Your data is stored on Google Firebase, which uses industry-standard encryption and security practices. We regularly review access controls and ensure that sensitive customer data is protected.
